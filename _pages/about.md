Hello! I'm Bowen Fang, currently studying Operations Research and Reinforcement Learning at Columbia University. I have a keen passion for mcts-based RL, enhancing heuristic operators with RL planning, and improving the efficiency of model-based RL.

My Interests
======
1. What really excites me about mcts-based RL is MuZero, which learns the dynamics(that are related to some features of interest) instead of having access to the simulator. And I am in favor of the idea that the algorithm can employ an inner policy improvement and evaluation process through heuristic search methods to improve the policy by minimizing the divergence of policy distribution and searched result. I am currently working on an open source mcts-based RL library, namely MUAX, to provide easy-to-use and easy-to-modify tools for RL practice. The goal of this library is to make it easy for users to test new algorithm designs and make it easy to integrate mcts-based algorithms into existing pipelines. I'm always eager to learn more about this topic, and I will continue to implement new algorithms proposed.
1. It is also my interest to seek for ways to design heuristic operators for general problem with specific constraints, and improve the heuristic operators by learning and planning methods. I would like to view heuristic operators as the functions of a robot, such as moving forward, left and right. While to solve a maze, planning would greatly guide the robot. I have been working on CVRP with constraint on pair-wise order of origins and destinations, and follow the idea of L2I to control the proposed operators with RL that employs planning.  

My Journey
======
I started my academic journey at Peking University, where I studied Big Data Management and Application. During this time, I developed a strong interest in Optimization and Reinforcement Learning, which led me to join the Knowledge Discovery Lab and do research under the goal-based RL topics, during which time I also became an intern for high frequency trading startup AI Topia to seek ways to optimize the execution of orders.

After completing my bachelor's degree, I decided to further explore this interest at IEOR department of Columbia University, where I am currently studying. I'm particularly focused on mcts-based RL and its applications on different tasks, for instance, MUAX, RLORSolver and Neural Tree Search(for active SLAM).
